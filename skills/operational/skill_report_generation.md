# Skill - Report Generation Standards

# skill_report_generation.md

---

# Purpose

This skill defines the executive report generation standards for the HYMIND project.

The goal is to ensure that all generated reports remain:

- Professional
- Structured
- Strategic
- Executive readable
- Reliable
- Actionable

This skill applies to:

- Weekly executive reports
- Technology trend reports
- Competitor intelligence reports
- Strategic alerts
- Future enterprise reporting workflows

---

# Core Philosophy

Reports are not simple summaries.

The purpose of HYMIND reports is to transform large volumes of fragmented industry information into structured executive intelligence.

Reports should help decision makers quickly understand:

- What happened
- Why it matters
- What changed
- What may happen next
- What requires attention

---

# Executive Readability Principle

All reports must remain executive readable.

The writing style should remain:

- Clear
- Structured
- Concise
- Strategic
- Informative
- Professional

The system should avoid:

- Excessive technical jargon
- Unstructured text walls
- Generic AI language
- Repetitive phrasing
- Overly academic writing

---

# Preferred Report Structure

Preferred executive report structure:

```text
Title
Executive Summary
Key Developments
Technology Updates
Competitor Activity
Policy & Funding Updates
Strategic Analysis
Key Risks & Opportunities
Conclusion
Sources
```

---

# Executive Summary Standards

The executive summary is the most important section.

It should:

- Summarize major developments
- Highlight strategic importance
- Identify market movement
- Remain concise
- Be understandable within 1-2 minutes

The executive summary should not exceed approximately:

```text
150 to 300 words
```

---

# Section Standards

Each section should:

- Focus on one topic area
- Remain logically structured
- Prioritize relevance
- Avoid redundancy
- Support fast scanning

Preferred structure:

```text
Headline
Short explanation
Strategic implication
```

---

# Strategic Analysis Philosophy

The system should not only summarize information.

It should identify:

- Market implications
- Technology direction
- Strategic significance
- Emerging patterns
- Competitive positioning
- Industry momentum

The analysis layer creates the real value of the platform.

---

# Technology Update Standards

Technology sections should focus on:

- Fuel cell developments
- Electrolyzer advancements
- Hydrogen infrastructure
- Energy storage innovation
- Industrial deployment
- Engineering breakthroughs

Technology updates should remain business relevant.

---

# Competitor Intelligence Standards

Competitor reporting should prioritize:

- Strategic partnerships
- Expansion activity
- Technology announcements
- Hiring trends
- Funding activity
- Manufacturing expansion
- Market positioning

Minor irrelevant updates should be filtered.

---

# Policy & Funding Standards

Policy sections should include:

- National hydrogen strategies
- Government funding programs
- EU hydrogen initiatives
- Regulatory developments
- Infrastructure incentives
- Political market impacts

Funding analysis is considered strategically important.

---

# Source Attribution Standards

Reports should remain traceable.

Whenever possible, reports should include:

- Source names
- URLs
- Publication references
- Dates

The system should avoid unsupported claims.

---

# Citation Philosophy

Source traceability improves:

- Credibility
- Reliability
- Auditability
- Strategic trustworthiness

Executive users must understand where conclusions originate.

---

# Formatting Standards

Preferred formatting:

- Markdown first
- Clear section headers
- Bullet points where useful
- Readable spacing
- Structured hierarchy

The formatting should support future PDF export cleanly.

---

# Tone Standards

The report tone should remain:

- Professional
- Strategic
- Neutral
- Informative
- Executive oriented

The system should avoid:

- Emotional language
- Marketing tone
- Speculative exaggeration
- Excessive certainty

---

# AI Writing Restrictions

The system should avoid obvious AI generated phrasing.

Examples to avoid:

```text
"In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
"It is important to note..."
"This highlights the significance..."
```

Reports should sound natural and analytical.

---

# Reliability Standards

Reports must avoid:

- Hallucinated claims
- Unsupported analysis
- Fake sources
- Invalid conclusions
- Duplicate information

Low confidence information should remain clearly framed.

---

# Alert Report Standards

Urgent alerts should remain:

- Short
- Actionable
- High priority focused
- Immediately understandable

Preferred structure:

```text
Alert
Why It Matters
Potential Impact
Recommended Attention
Source
```

---

# Trend Analysis Standards

Trend reports should focus on:

- Long term developments
- Recurring market movement
- Technology momentum
- Regional investment patterns
- Strategic positioning changes

Trend reports should synthesize multiple sources over time.

---

# Report Modularity Principles

Report generation should remain modular.

Future sections may include:

- Patent intelligence
- Internal company knowledge
- SharePoint insights
- Financial analysis
- Risk scoring
- Predictive trend modeling

The architecture should support expansion without redesign.

---

# Future Distribution Philosophy

Reports may later support:

- Markdown export
- PDF generation
- Telegram delivery
- Gmail distribution
- Teams notifications
- Dashboard integration

Formatting consistency must support all future delivery channels.

---

# Quality Validation Standards

Before finalization, reports should validate:

- Required sections exist
- No empty sections remain
- Sources exist
- Duplicates removed
- Formatting valid
- Executive summary present

Incomplete reports should not be distributed automatically.

---

# Reliability Philosophy

Executive reporting is the final intelligence layer of the HYMIND platform.

Report quality directly reflects system credibility.

The project prioritizes:

- Strategic clarity
- Reliability
- Traceability
- Readability
- Consistency

over excessive verbosity.

---

# Operational Engineering Principle

Report generation is treated as a strategic intelligence capability.

The output should resemble professional executive industry intelligence rather than generic AI generated summaries.
