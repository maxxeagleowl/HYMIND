## Risks and Watchouts
- **Infrastructure gap remains the dominant adoption barrier.** Hydrogen refuelling density in most European and US markets cannot support consumer FCEV adoption at scale. The 2030 AFIR targets require a 6-8x increase in station count.
- **BEV dominance in consumer segments.** Battery electric vehicle cost reductions and charging network expansion continue to compress the addressable market for consumer FCEVs in Europe and North America.
- **Chinese OEM cost competition.** SAIC and Dongfeng commercial FCEV products priced 30–40% below Western competitors. If quality perception improves and CE certification is achieved, European commercial fleet procurement could shift.
- **Fuel cell stack cost roadmap delays.** Neither Toyota nor Hyundai has publicly met previously stated $60/kW cost targets. Each year of delay extends the period during which FCEVs remain premium-priced relative to BEV equivalents.
- **Green hydrogen availability for refuelling.** Most hydrogen refuelling stations continue to use grey hydrogen. Consumer and fleet ESG requirements for green hydrogen certification add supply chain complexity and cost.
